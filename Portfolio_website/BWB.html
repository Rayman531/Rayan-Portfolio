Blended Wing Body Unmanned Aerial Vehicle

Fourth-Year Capstone Project | Carleton University

Project Overview

The Blended Wing Body UAV is a fourth-year Capstone project at Carleton University, focused on designing and manufacturing a solar-powered aircraft surveillance system for remote powerline surveillance and mapping.

I led the design of a modular 3D-printed wing system capable of being easily assembled and disassembled. I worked closely with the Masterlines, Manufacturing, and Stress teams to ensure all design, structural, and assembly requirements were met.

Outcomes and Contributions

Modular Wing Design

I developed a modular wing design to enable 3D-printed manufacturing and on-site assembly.

I collaborated with the Stress team to analyze internal load paths and stress concentrations, then used Fusion 360 to segment each 3-foot wing into 7 structurally sound, printable sections. I also designed internal ribs and spars to ensure structural integrity and load transfer between segments.

Result: Enabled structurally sound segmentation aligned with mechanical performance requirements.

Interlocking and Fastening Interfaces

I determined interlocking and fastening interfaces consisting of modified I-beam spar structures for modular assembly.

I designed mating features and employed M3 heat-set inserts with single-sided access counterbore screws to simplify assembly and disassembly, and reduce parasitic drag. Throughout the design process i made sure to integrate design-for-manufacturability (DFM) and geometric dimensioning and tolerancing (GD&T) principles to ensure consistent fit and alignment of screws and interfacing parts.

Result: Created a repeatable and field-serviceable assembly method that reduced build complexity and increased reliability of connections.

Component Validation

I validated printability and structural fit of redesigned components through half scale models and rapid prototyping.

I oversaw part export and 3D printing, then led the full wing assembly process to verify alignment, tolerance stack-up, and mechanical fit. During this procecces the elevon system was also itegrated into the wing as well as fastening the wing to the fusealge.

Result: Successfully built and tested a fully modular wing prototype, confirming elevon function and performing a taxi test.
