PID Ball Balance Controller

Project Overview

Following my Feedback and Control Systems course i decided to work on a PID project to futher solidify my understanding of PID controllers and feedback systems.

This project invloved a 3D printed scale with the objective of balancing a ping pong ball at its center though the use of PID control. The feedback system consisted of and infrared sensor which detected the ball's distance, it then relayed that information through an Arduino Uno microcontroller which calculated the error, based on this the PID controller would adjust the position of a servo motor to ttilt the scale and move the ball back to its center.

Outcomes and Contributions

CAD and Assembly

Fusion360 was used to design all the parts for the scale, the components were then 3D printed using ABS

The balance was assembled using various screws, glue and tape, ensuring all hinges rotated smoothly.

Result: All parts successfuly 3D printed and assembled.

Circuit Setup

The circuit consisted of a servo motor and an infrared (IR) sensor connected to an Arduino Uno microcontroller.

The servo was attached to digital pin D9, which supports Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) to control the motor's position by varying the pulse width of the signal. The IR sensor was connected to analog pin A0, as it outputs a continuous analog voltage corresponding to the position of the ball.

Result: Circuit was assembled and system compments were tested to ensure function before being implemented into the full assembly

Arduino Code

The code implements a PID control system to stabalize the ball by adjusting the postion of the servo motor based on the distance measurments from the IR sensor

The PID components consisted of:

  • Proportional (PID_p): The proportional component is responsible for the immediate response to the error. It adjusts the postion of the ball on the current error value.
  • Integral (PID_i): The integral component accumulates the error over time, addressing any persistent offset of the ball position that may not be corrected by the proportional component alone.
  • Derivative (PID_d): The derivative component predicts future error based onthe speed of the ball, providing a damping effect to reduce overshoot of the ball's postion.

    • The controller was tuned through trial and error by tweaking the proportional, derivative and intergral constants (kp, ki, kd)

    The error in this case is the difference between the desired setpoint (middle of the scale) and the actual distance of the ball
